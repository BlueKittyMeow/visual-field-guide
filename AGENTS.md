# Visual Field Guide — Agent Instructions

This repository develops Lara's personal visual identity as a living field guide rather than a conventional commercial brand.

## Core mode

The base visual language is:

- scholarly and archival
- specimen-oriented and taxonomic
- domestic and handmade
- optical and scientific, not theatrical
- tactile, materially specific, and substantially fin-de-siècle
- intimate, observant, ordered, and quietly obsessive

Recurring material may include:

- small kraft-paper specimen boxes, including cigar-box-sized shell collections sorted by size
- handwritten, typed, stamped, or carefully lettered labels
- shallow drawers, compartments, envelopes, cards, and filing systems
- paper, old cardboard, book cloth, linen, thread, glass, brass, shell, bone, and waxed wood
- optical instruments and image-making apparatus
- natural-history study undertaken in a domestic setting
- signs of handling, repair, annotation, use, and care

## Uncanny mode

The uncanny is an optional overlay applied to the established world. It is not the base identity.

Possible transformations include:

- dimming or narrowing light
- optical ghosting and doubled registration
- print misalignment
- contamination, foxing, oxidation, bloom, and material decay
- partial illegibility
- taxonomy beginning to fail
- subtle duplication, displacement, or distortion
- order slipping without becoming theatrical horror

## Avoid

Do not collapse the project into:

- generic dark academia
- theatrical Gothic
- steampunk
- commercial witchcore
- Halloween imagery
- generic sepia antiquarian styling
- luxury heritage branding
- sterile minimalist museum branding
- occult symbolism used as an aesthetic shortcut
- costume-drama excess

## Working principles

1. Do not categorize prematurely. Allow stable groups to emerge from collected material.
2. Preserve source information and descriptive metadata whenever practical.
3. Prefer precise material and compositional language over broad aesthetic labels.
4. Keep the core identity distinct from effects applied in uncanny mode.
5. Treat visual references as inspiration unless explicitly marked as production assets.
6. Record meaningful acceptance and rejection decisions in `catalog/decisions.md`.
7. Do not rename or destructively reorganize source images without preserving originals.
