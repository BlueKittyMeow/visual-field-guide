# Analogue image shortlist

These are personal photographs and analogue experiments selected from the first submitted batch. They are not the whole visual identity. They define how the formal archival world may behave under optical, chemical, mnemonic, and uncanny pressure.

## Architectural principle

The future personal landing page should remain formally archival, scholarly, materially grounded, and substantially fin-de-siècle in its structure. Analogue optics operate across that structure as an active surface: flare, veiling, chromatic drift, sprocket traces, grain, double exposure, and imperfect registration. The uncanny should remain concentrated at thresholds, edges, corners, transitions, and deeper pages rather than compromising basic legibility.

## Current interpretation

The photographs reveal an interest not only in archived objects but in the material instability of seeing. Light is treated as an event: it stains, veils, burns, doubles, erases, and reveals. Images remain partly legible rather than becoming pure abstraction. This suggests a three-part visual system:

1. **Formal archive** — scholarly, specimen-oriented, domestic, and fin-de-siècle.
2. **Optical transformation** — analogue process visible across the surface without destroying order.
3. **Uncanny depth** — concentrated instability at margins, thresholds, and deeper layers.
