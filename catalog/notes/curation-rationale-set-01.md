# Curation Rationale Set 01

## Formal identity

The base visual system is:

* archival
* scholarly
* materially historical
* domestic
* specimen-oriented
* substantially fin-de-siecle
* formally organized
* trustworthy and legible

## Optical layer

The active visual surface includes:

* violet, blue, magenta, apricot, amber, and teal chromatic drift
* light leaks
* veiling glare
* translucent curtains and glass
* reflections
* apertures
* projection-like light
* film edges
* grain
* registration shifts
* analogue image behavior

## Uncanny layer

The uncanny should appear primarily through:

* edges
* corners
* thresholds
* shadows
* deeper pages
* optical distortion
* image treatment
* atmosphere
* subtle degradation

It should not rely primarily on:

* occult imagery
* hooded figures
* ritual rooms
* gothic horror shorthand
* corrupted factual records
* false captions
* duplicated database records

## Trust principle

The archive's information remains accurate.

Strangeness belongs in presentation, not in falsifying the record.

## Provenance principle

Lara's analogue photographs are original creative source material and carry greater identity weight than externally collected inspiration references.
