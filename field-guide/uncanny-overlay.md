# Uncanny Overlay

## Principle

The uncanny is not a separate aesthetic pasted onto the project. It is a changed condition of the established archive.

The same drawers, labels, shells, papers, lenses, and worktables remain present, but their systems begin to misbehave. Light narrows. Registration slips. Categories become unreliable. Materials bloom, ghost, stain, or repeat. Something has happened to the act of seeing and sorting.

## Possible transformations

### Light

- dimming ambient light
- isolated illumination from a lens, projection source, or narrow window
- falloff that hides the edges of a collection
- weak greenish or mineral fluorescence used sparingly

### Image and registration

- doubled contours
- chromatic or mechanical misregistration
- partial exposure
- optical flare or internal reflection
- blurred edges beside areas of unnatural precision
- repetition with slight discrepancies

### Material degradation

- foxing and tide marks
- oxidation
- mold-like bloom without becoming gore or horror texture
- warped paper and lifting labels
- cracked emulsion or damaged glass
- softened ink, erased notation, and adhesive residue

### Taxonomic failure

- compartments that no longer match their labels
- sequences with missing or duplicated numbers
- objects subtly sorted by an unknowable criterion
- handwriting changing within a single system
- specimens that appear displaced between views
- careful order persisting after its rationale has become inaccessible

## Emotional register

The effect should be quiet, intimate, and destabilizing rather than loudly frightening. It may suggest memory failure, damaged transmission, environmental contamination, perceptual slippage, or an archive becoming animate—but should not settle too quickly into a single explanation.

## Avoid

- horror-film lighting
- obvious monsters or apparitions
- decorative skull-and-candle Gothic
- gratuitous occult signs
- gore, viscera, or sensational decay
- glitch effects detached from physical media
- a green wash applied indiscriminately
- chaos that erases the underlying intelligence of the core world

## Test

An uncanny treatment belongs when a viewer could first recognize the ordinary archival system beneath it, then feel that the system has become subtly unreliable.
