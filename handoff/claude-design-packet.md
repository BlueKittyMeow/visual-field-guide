# Claude Design Exploration Packet

## Purpose

Generate three genuinely different visual identity and landing-page directions for Lara's personal website.

The website should be formally archival, scholarly, materially historical, and substantially fin-de-siecle. The underlying information architecture must remain trustworthy, legible, and useful. Optical and analogue effects operate over that formal structure. Uncanniness should be restrained and emerge at edges, thresholds, corners, shadows, image treatments, and deeper pages.

This is an exploration pass. Claude should propose typography, palette, composition, spacing, image treatment, ornament, and motion rather than merely executing a fully predetermined system.

## Core visual model

### Base structure

- scholarly archive
- private collection
- specimen culture
- domestic handwork
- optical and scientific apparatus
- late nineteenth-century material life
- cabinets, cards, labels, shallow drawers, workrooms, curtains, glass, paper, wood, linen, brass
- formality without institutional coldness
- historical resonance without reenactment

### Optical layer

- analogue photographic behavior
- violet, blue, magenta, teal, amber, apricot, and cream
- veiling glare
- light leaks
- reflections
- translucent curtains and glass
- apertures
- grain
- film edges
- chromatic drift
- projected or refracted light

### Uncanny layer

- subtle darkening at margins
- doubled or displaced presentation layers
- partial obscurity
- threshold states
- atmospheric depth
- restrained degradation
- strangeness in presentation, never false data

## Hard exclusions

Do not use:

- generic dark academia
- Halloween gothic
- occult or ritual imagery
- hooded figures
- steampunk
- commercial witchcore
- fake archival captions
- duplicated records as a gimmick
- false metadata
- generic sepia antiquarian styling
- luxury heritage branding
- sterile museum minimalism
- AI-gothic character art
- excessive ornamental Victorian pastiche

## Required outputs

Create three distinct visual directions:

1. **The Reading Room**
   - most formal and typographic
   - restrained optical behavior
   - scholarly, architectural, quietly intimate

2. **The Optical Cabinet**
   - stronger analogue and refractive treatment
   - apertures, glass, projected light, chromatic drift
   - still orderly and usable

3. **The Field Register**
   - most domestic, specimen-like, tactile, and personal
   - kraft surfaces, labels, drawers, shells, botanical material, handwriting or utilitarian notation used carefully

For each direction, propose:

- primary and secondary typefaces, with reasons
- type scale and hierarchy
- palette with named swatches and hex values
- grid, spacing, margins, and density
- image treatment rules
- rules, borders, labels, captions, and ornamental details
- optical effects and motion behaviors
- how the uncanny appears without disrupting trust or usability
- desktop landing page
- mobile landing page
- one deeper project/archive page
- one compact identity sheet summarizing the system

Also include a comparison page explaining what each direction emphasizes and where it risks becoming generic or over-literal.

## Content assumptions

The landing page is for a technically skilled, scholarly, creative person whose work spans:

- writing and literary research
- archives, libraries, media, and digital systems
- software and experimental technical projects
- image-making and analogue photographic work
- natural-history, optical, and material-culture interests

Use placeholder content sparingly. Keep the page personal rather than corporate. Avoid conventional startup or agency language.

## Trust principle

The data and labels are accurate. The site may feel strange through light, layering, image behavior, texture, and transitions, but should never imply that its records are unreliable.

## Exploration freedom

Claude is specifically invited to propose typography, palette refinement, composition, spacing, and visual-system ideas not already specified here. Do not simply imitate the reference images literally. Infer a coherent system from them.

## Reference-image hierarchy

Treat Lara's personal analogue photographs as the strongest evidence of the optical layer. External images are supporting references rather than templates.

Prioritize images that show:

- inhabited scholarly rooms
- domestic optical apparatus
- translucent curtains and reflective glass
- small historic houses and coastal structures
- fog, thresholds, corridors, water, shadow
- violet, magenta, blue-green, amber, cream, wood, kraft, and faded black

Do not use quarantined literal-gothic references.
